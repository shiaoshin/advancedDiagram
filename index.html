Advanced Diagramming
2014 Spring | Tomoko Ichikawa

This six-week class explored and applied dynamic and interactive qualities to create understandable, effective diagrams and visualizations. We reviewed current theories and examined real-world examples of narratives that use data visualizations, analyzing dynamic properties that can enhance comprehension and exploration of the content.

Students were challenged to create rough prototypes of visualizations, trying different techniques to demonstrate their concept. The rough nature of the prototypes helped place focus iterating their dynamic visualizations rather than the visual polish of the final design. They used various digital tools from interactive InDesign, Google Fusion Table, marvel app and HTML5/CSS.
